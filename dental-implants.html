Dental Implants Overview

Dental implants are synthetic titanium-made tooth roots inserted (implanted) in the jawbone (endosteal) or above the jawbone (subperiosteal) instead of a missing tooth root. Upon these implants, dental crowns are placed, hence called: implant-supported crowns. With the correct application, our dentists will ensure perfect teeth replacement, restoration and jawbone structure.

Inside & Outside the Clinic

Perhaps due to a root canal treatment or tooth extraction, the root couldn't be salvaged and was removed. Trauma like car accidents may also damage the tooth, calling for either of the previous treatments. And while dental bridges replace missing teeth, you might need an implant if necessary.

Before Procedure

Not all patients at Barad Dental with missing tooth roots, are advised with dental implants. If our patients maintained an agreeable oral health and possess suitable jawbone density, our dentists may proceed with an implant treatment. Before the operation, we will explain the alternative options like bridges and dentures.

During Procedure

If we surmised that implants were the best option, restorations attached to the implants will be discussed whether you desire crowns, bridges, or dentures.

At the outset, Barad Dental's team will strong for any damaged or decayed teeth in the operation site and extract them before proceeding to jawbone preparation. Albeit the sufficient jawbone density, sometimes, the patient needs further bone fortification to support the implant. Transplanting bone can be performed from one part of the body for instance from the chin to the jaw — an autograft — or from a bone tissue donor — an allograft. Either way, bone grafting isn't performed unless necessity calls for it.
A titanium fixture or post is surgically inserted into the jawbone and the gums are stitched together. To continue the process, the implant should merge with the patient's surrounding bones which takes around 2-3 months. Osseointegration provides more stability for the implant and once completed, our dentist connects the implant to the restorative structure(crown, bridge, denture) with an abutment (connector). For a custom-made dental restoration, molds of the implant and the surrounding area are taken using 3D digital technology and sent out to dental laboratories for a final restorative treatment.

After Procedure

For the best results, Barad Dental suggests your continuous follow-up and good oral hygiene.
Dental Implant Surgery may result in gum infection, operation failure and/or serious nerve damage if you don't choose your dentist wisely. With Barad Dental's services, you can rest assured that you will recieve the best care you ever practiced at the hands of our dentists.

Frequently Asked Questions

Bridges, Crowns and Implants: FAQ's

What's the difference between bridges & crowns?

Bridges replace one or more teeth while a crown is a cap that covers and protects the entire tooth. Apart from their obvious purpose, bridges consist of one or more artificial teeth that are placed over crowns and/or implants. Crowns are tooth restorations that fit over the entire tooth spanning all sides starting from the gumline.
In contrast to bridges that restore chewing/ speaking abilities and prevent teeth from tilting towards missing tooth spaces, crowns protect a particular tooth from decay and strengthen them.

What's the difference between Crowns & Implants?

Implants are artificial tooth roots usually made from titanium that are placed (implanted) into the jawbone instead of a missing tooth root. Above these titanium-made implants or a salvaged natural tooth, a golden or ceramic crown is placed.

When does my dentist use crowns or bridges?

Dr. Karam Zagzoog differentiates between their main functions, "Usually, we use crowns after endo-treatment or for aesthetic reasons. On the other hand, we use the bridges if we have missing teeth. For example: if we did an extraction for one tooth, we need to fill that empty space so using a bridge."
