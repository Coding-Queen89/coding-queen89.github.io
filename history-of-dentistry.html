WELCOME TO THE HISTORY OF DENTISTRY!

Dental Pracice had been around for as long as 5000 years from the Ancient Egyptians to the Chinese! Explore how mesmerizing humans have evolved throughout the centuries!

3000 B.C.

The Ancient Egyptians used golden wires to align teeth and had recipes for toothaches on manuscripts describing "tooth worms"...

622 A.D.

1400 years ago, Prophet Mohammed, PBUH, advised Muslims to brush their teeth with "miswak" before each of their five prayers. Miswak contains antibacterial and anit-inflamatory properties...

1025 A.D.

Avicenna or Ibn Sina deduced that a jaw fracture should be reduced according to the occlusal guidance of teeth, a deduction still in use today! And Al-Zahrawi's 200 surgical tools are similar to what we, dentists, use today...

1462 A.D.

In the Middle Ages, barbers used to perform dental extractions and minor surgeries including applying leaches and fixing broken bones...

1530 A.D.

It was in 1530 A.D when the first dental book, "Artzney Buchlein", was published in German and Latin, and the "Operator for the Teeth" was the first English book written by Charles Allen a century into the future...

1728 A.D.

The Father of Modern Dentistry, Pierre Fauchard, proposed dental fillings to cure cavities and ivory or bone to replace lost teeth. His correct deductions on tumors surrounding teeth and dental decay crowned him the pioneer...

1879 A.D.

Dentistry flourished after Pierre Fauchard. In 1879, young Francis Brodie was the first dentist elected as president in his a medical college. Dentistry challenged medicine for the first time in history...

1955 A.D.

Dentists, now recognized and respected, observed a decline in tooth cavities in locations where water naturally contained increased levels of flouride. In 1955, the first flouride toothpaste, Crest, was manufactured and tested...

2000 A.D. - FUTURE

In the 21st century, Invisalign replaced metal braces, 3D printing was introduced and CRISPR/CAS-9 caused an ultimate reduction in periodontal diseases. The future is bright...
