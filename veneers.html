Brighten your Smile!

You keep hearing that word...veneers. But what are they? What do they do? When and why do you need them?
In short, veneers are thin shells that cover your front teeth and our dentists fix them to place so that you can't take them off. Veneers can be made up of porcelain (ceramic) or composite resins (plastic and glass) and we usually use them to close gaps between teeth and improve shape of teeth to name a few.

Direct Veneers

When it comes to choosing between direct or indirect veneers, the material is identical for both: composite resins.
The difference is based on the procedure of application. In direct veneers, the composite resin is applied directly to your teeth. Your teeth are prepared and ready in a matter of minutes for the application. Though direct veneers are minimal in their cost, they are usually flawed when compared to indirect veneers.

Indirect Veneers

Indirect composite veneers are made to last! Your teeth are prepared the same way as they would for direct veneers, but the veneers are made ‘indirectly’ outside of the mouth in Barad Dental's laboratory.
You’ll receive a set of temporary veneers until the indirect veneers are made. At the next appointment, the indirect composite veneers are applied on your teeth with a layer of adhesive. Indirect composite veneers can withstand more chafing and corrosion. They resist fractures better than the direct version. Some of our patients also try on the hollywood smile for a stronger, more confident laugh!

When do I need them?

Veneers aren't for everyone. We usually apply veneers for patients who have gaps between their teeth, and don't prefer applying braces or Invisalign with our orthodonists. Since veneers are a highly cosmetical service, our dentists utilize them for improving the color of all or some teeth. Furthermore, some patients, like the one shown in the photo to the left, need a correction of fractures and/or erosion caused by a rather poor dental hygiene. Barad Dental combines crowns and veneers to create a wonderful smile!

Complications of Veneers

Both composite and porcelain veneers have some risks in the long term. For example...

  • Composite veneers look less realistic than porcelain veneers which are translucent like natural teeth.
  • Both types of veneers are irreversible, thus we highly recommend our patients to think over their choices.
  • Composite and porcelain veneers might cause tooth sensitivity in the long term which might be uncomfortable.
  • Patients who grind strongly or clench their teeth are adviced to go for composite veneers as the porcelain coating might crack.
