What are Night Guards?

Your bedtime hours should be the most blissful time of your day. If you're stressed before falling asleep or take too much caffeine you might notice yourself clenching your jaw and unintentionally damaging your teeth. Night guards are dental durable flexible appliances fit to your mouth to prevent the upper and lower teeth from clenching. They take in the force of the jaw grind thereby decreasing possible tooth fractures and breakage. Other reasons that you might be clenching your jaw unwillingly are Temporo-Mandibular Joint Disorder (TMJ), teeth misalignment, and/or poor sleeping positions.

Bruxism & Temporo-Mandibular Joints

Bruxism is a condition addressed with teeth grinding during sleep or daytime (awake bruxism). It's most common in the early years of teens and young adulthood. Although the cause of bruxism is still vague as of 2024, it is believed to be a blend of psychological, physiological and lifestyle factors. If left untreated, bruxism may lead to Temporo-Mandibular Joint Disorder.

The joint that connects the jaws (mandibles) with the left & right parts of the skull (temporalis) is called the Temporo-Mandibular Joint. Sometimes, and for unknown reasons, the joint and nearby muscles malfunction causing severe jaw pain when chewing. "The joint helps in yawning and jaw movements," Dr. Ayham Ammoura, a periodontal surgeon with microbiology & periodontal disease researches, draws attention to one scenario where he was in a conversation with a close friend when the latter yawned and couldn't close his jaw. "To return the jaw to its starting position, I pulled it further downwards then back. One wrong move would've spelled disaster."

Why are Night Guards useful?

Night guards which are often made of thermoplastic material prevent you from clenching your jaw (bruxism) while you're asleep. Thus, the flexible mouth guards will: , ease your jaw pain, improve sleep quality and might prevent TMJ from developing.

  • Protect your tooth restorations
  • Ease you jaw pain
  • Improve sleep quality
  • Prevent TMJ from developing

Types of Night Guards

Although you can find mouth guards over-the-counter, they are often not as comfortable and beneficial as custom-made guards in Barad Dental where impressions of your mouth are taken with advanced digital technology and acrylic molds are shaped to fit your teeth set precisely. The acrylic material is used for patients who experience severe bruxism and/or TMJ problems whilst thermoplastic (PVC) serves those who have mild symptoms.

Usage & Care Tips

Before bedtime, brush your teeth thoroughly for at least 2 minutes prior to applying the night guard and an extra bonus for cleaning your guard as well. After each use, make sure you gently brush your guard in the morning and store it back in its case. When you rinse your appliance, use lukewarm water, avoiding hot temperatures as that could distort its shape.
