Root Canal Treatment Introduction

Your teeth are your body's gleaming diamonds and you should not take them for granted! When you do, bacteria and micro-organisms invade your mouth and start feeding on the leftover food in your teeth. As they feed, they release acids that breakdown your enamel. The Root Canal Treatment(RCT) is a procedure used to treat this damage within the tooth's pulp chamber and root canals.

Pulp Damage

A tooth pulp contains nerves, veins, arteries and connective tissue making the tooth alive. When bacteria find its way to the pulp, your body's immune cells will fight the infection, resulting in an inflammation of the pulp.

Causes & Effects

But dental cavities, though very dangerous, aren't the only cause of pulp damage. "Inflammation is a body's immune response in the face of different irritating factors," Dr. Ayham Ammoura, a professional periodontist certified with 4 years of periodontology in Giessen, Germany, shines a light on multiple irritaing factors. "The most famous of them being caries, but also trauma, temperature irritation and others." Cracks in the tooth enamel due to harsh impacts, for example, allow bacteria to enter through the fracture to the pulp.
If left untreated, and while the tooth is still alive, severe toothache will persist as long as the issue is not addressed. Death of the tooth from the lack of blood supply and neglect will cause discoloration of the tooth. The problem doesn't end there! The infection will spread to the surrounding tissues leading to serious jawbone infections that might call for immediate medical care. Systemic health risks like poor pregnancy outcomes, diabetes and/or cardiovascular disease might originate from bacteria that has travelled from the tooth pulp through blood vessels into various body organs.

Signs and Symptoms

Does your tooth hurt when you touch it? Don't worry, it might be some mild sensitivity that will go away with time. However, if the problem continues, Barad's Dentists might diagnose you with pulpitis (inflamed tooth), according to the following signs and symptoms:

  • Swelling: If the inflammation developed to a dental abscess, your gums and/or face may swell.
  • Pain: when biting on the tooth or tapping down. Your head, face and/or jaw might also pulse with pain from the tooth.
  • Pain Relief: Temporary decline in pain might be due to the death of the tooth(necrosis).

Root Canal Treatment

The natural hollows within a tooth protectively sheltering the nerve tissue, blood vessels & connective tissue is called a root canal. Endodontic Therapy or Root Canal Treatment(RCT) is a treatment procedure of the infected tooth pulp in hopes of eliminating the infection and protecting the decontaminated tooth from future infections.

RCT Procedure

Barad Dental's dentists will assess the tooth for diagnosis. "First of all, we check the vitality of the nerve inside the tooth. There are different techniques we use. One of them is the cold test." Dr. Karam Zagzoog attained premier certifications from Liverpool, England and describes the RCT process he undertakes with his fellow partners. The cold test, he elaborates, ascertains whether the pulp is necrotic (dead) or not by the presence or absence of any response like sensitivity or discomfort.
If RCT is the best option, we will sedate your tooth with local anesthesia and surrounding tissues to ensure your absolute comfort during the procedure.

Isolating the tooth from oral cavity will help keep it dry from saliva and bacteria during the operation. RCT is realistically initiated with access opening. Barad's dentists create an opening in the crown of the tooth to access its pulp. This may include drilling through existing fillings and/or crowns.
"At that point," Dr. Karam relays. "We start by cleaning and shaping the canals with special instruments." The damaged pulp tissue is removed along with bacteria and debris. Utilizing larger files (dental instruments), the dentist will shape & smooth the root canals to prevent further bacteria from entering and re-infecting the pulp. Barad Dental takes immense care when filling and sealing the root canals like no other clinic in KSA. We ensure that our patients will never complain about that tooth again. The biocompatible material gutta-percha along with a sealer help us block the canals completely.
On the outside, the tooth will be restored with dental fillings, or crowns to fortify its structure.

At Barad Dental, we've had multiple patients from other clinics with faulty tooth restorations and procedures. NONE of our patients however left our clinic for another or came back to us after a procedure. If you are still afraid of a Root Canal Treatment, it has more advantages than not:
